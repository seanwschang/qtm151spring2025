(d) Flag subset of data and plot

"growth_euro" had a value greater than 2 or less than -2

  • Generate a line plot with the "growth_euro" series, label the axes and the title
  • Use the command plt.fill_between() as in Lecture 18 to create a shaded region

# Write your own code

currencies['bool_cond'] = (currencies['growth_euro'] > 2) | (currencies['growth_euro'] < -2)

plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))
plt.plot(currencies['date_string'], currencies['growth_euro'], label="Growth Rate of Euro to USD", color='blue')

plt.fill_between(x=currencies['date_string'], 
                 y1=currencies['growth_euro'].min(), 
                 y2=currencies['growth_euro'].max(), 
                 where=currencies['bool_cond'], 
                 alpha=0.2, color='red', label="Significant Growth (>2% or <-2%)")

plt.xlabel("Date")
plt.ylabel("Daily Percentage Change")
plt.title("Daily Growth Rate of Euro to USD with Significant Changes")
plt.legend()
plt.show()
